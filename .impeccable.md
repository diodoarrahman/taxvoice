## Design Context

### Users
Indonesian students and young adults (18–30), primarily university students learning civic responsibility. They access TaxVoice casually on laptops and phones, likely during study breaks or class. They're curious but not policy experts — they need complexity made approachable. They care about Indonesia's future but may not yet have fully paid taxes themselves.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy & civic · Educational & clear · Bold & activist

Three-word distillation: **urgent, legible, civic**

Emotional goal: make young Indonesians feel that understanding taxes is empowering, not intimidating. The interface should feel like a confident teacher — not a bureaucrat, not a startup.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Navy blue + clean refinement. Not a rebrand — a quality lift. The current navy (#1e3a8a) palette is correct; execution needs polish.
- **Theme**: Light mode. Students using this on campus, in daylight, on mixed devices.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid government-website stuffiness, avoid startup purple-to-blue gradients and glassmorphism, avoid the "data dashboard with glowing metrics" aesthetic.
- **References**: Think civic journalism (Indonesia Corruption Watch, good government portals), clear editorial data presentation, confident public institution communication.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — every screen should be instantly readable; no design element should require explanation.
2. **Data as civic empowerment** — numbers and charts should feel revelatory, not intimidating. Context always accompanies statistics.
3. **Institutional confidence** — the interface carries authority through precision and restraint, not decorative complexity.
4. **Mobile-first for students** — touch targets, readable type, and key flows must work perfectly on a 375px screen.
5. **Bold where it counts** — save visual weight for calls-to-action and key data points; let everything else breathe.
